Our Story

A Silent
Revolution

"Have every child experience the inspiration and mysticism embodied in Indian and World heritage."

1972, Brooklyn Academy of Music

The "Big Bang" Moment

The seed of SPIC MACAY was sown not in India, but in New York. In 1972, a young Kiran Seth, then a doctoral student at Columbia University, attended a Dhrupad concert by the legendary Ustad Nasir Aminuddin Dagar and Ustad Zia Fariduddin Dagar at the Brooklyn Academy of Music.

He was expecting entertainment; he found transformation. The profound depth of the music struck a chord he didn't know existed. He realized that if he, an educated Indian, was unaware of this treasure, what about the millions of youth back home?

Returning to India as a professor at IIT Delhi, he gathered a few students in 1977. They started with meager funds but boundless zeal. Thus began a movement to conserve and promote an awareness of this rich and heterogeneous cultural tapestry amongst the youth of this country.

Our Symbol

The Eye of Wisdom

The SPIC MACAY logo represents the "Third Eye". In mythology, when the physical eyes are closed to the distractions of the world, the third eye opens to inner wisdom, perception, and enquiry.

"Outward growth is measurable while inward journey is endless."

We strive to take the student on this inward journey. In a world obsessed with external achievements, we offer a path to find the center within.

Our Ethos

Beyond Techniques

SPIC MACAY runs on the spirit of Nishkam Seva—service without expectation of reward. It is a volunteer movement where thousands give their time freely, not for certificates or money, but for the cause itself.

How We Work

Interaction with the Maestros

The Lecture-Demonstration

We go beyond the conventional style of concerts. Our unique format, the "Lecture-Demonstration" (Lec-Dem), creates an informal setting where the artist explains the grammar of the art form before performing.
